Beyond the Pilot: The Operating Playbook for AI-Era Banking

Finance Vietnam 2026 | Ho Chi Minh City

Register Now View Agenda

Vietnam's economy enters 2026 as one of Southeast Asia's most dynamic growth stories. The World Bank and ADB project GDP growth in the 6.5–7.0% range, underpinned by robust manufacturing exports, a surging FDI pipeline, accelerating digital consumption, and a government-backed push to position Vietnam as a regional fintech and digital infrastructure hub. Inflation remains contained, credit growth has reaccelerated across retail, MSME and housing segments, and the State Bank of Vietnam (SBV) has entered a measured easing cycle that is unlocking consumer and business demand. For banks, this is the most constructive operating environment in years — and also the moment at which competitive advantage is being fundamentally recast.

The Vietnamese banking sector now sits at the intersection of three large transitions running simultaneously. First, an AI transition that is shifting from isolated pilots to production workflows in fraud detection, KYC, collections, credit scoring and advisory, with material implications for cost-to-serve, headcount and revenue per customer. Second, a digital money transition, in which the SBV's CBDC pilot, regulated VND-linked stablecoins, and tokenised government securities are beginning to reframe what "bank money" means and how corporates, asset managers and retail customers use it. Third, a connectivity transition, anchored by Vietnam's integration into ASEAN cross-border QR, its bilateral corridors with Singapore, Thailand and South Korea, and the country's accelerating readiness for Project Nexus.

Set against this is a hardening risk environment. Deepfake-enabled identity fraud, mule-account networks, APP scams and increasingly sophisticated social engineering attacks have made scam-fraud a board-level P&L concern. Cloud and AI vendor concentration risk is now on CRO agendas. And customer trust — once an abstract value — has become quantifiable through reimbursement expectations, supervisory scrutiny and brand-level NPS pressure.

Finance Vietnam 2026 brings together the senior leaders, regulators and infrastructure providers writing the operating playbook for this new era. Conversations will be biased toward execution: what works, at what unit economics, with what controls, and with what regulator alignment. The agenda is built around operating domains — retail and wealth, SME and commercial banking, transaction banking and cross-border payments, digital money, and resilience and fraud — with AI, tokenisation and open finance treated as enablers inside each domain rather than as standalone headline topics.

Who will attend?

The programme is designed for senior executives from banks, digital banks, fintechs, regulators, infrastructure providers, and large corporates with treasury and merchant-acceptance footprints across Vietnam and ASEAN, including:

  • CEOs, presidents, country heads and managing directors of banks, digital banks and fintechs
  • CFOs, COOs and chief strategy officers shaping 2026–2027 productivity, cost-to-serve and capital allocation decisions
  • CIOs, CDOs, CTOs, heads of AI and data, and heads of digital banking
  • CROs, CISOs, heads of fraud and AML, and chief compliance officers
  • Heads of retail, mass affluent, wealth, private banking, and consumer credit
  • Heads of SME, commercial banking, transaction banking, payments and remittance
  • Treasurers and CFOs of corporates with USD/multi-currency exposure
  • Senior officials from SBV, Ministry of Finance, Ministry of Information and Communications, and ASEAN counterparts
  • Strategic partners and infrastructure providers in payments, identity, cloud, AI, fraud and core banking
