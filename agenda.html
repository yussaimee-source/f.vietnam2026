The Agenda

Finance Vietnam 2026 | Thursday, 11 June 2026 | Ho Chi Minh City

08:00 – 09:00

Registration and Morning Networking

09:00 – 10:15
Keynote

Opening Remarks & International Keynote

From pilot to P&L: the new operating economics of AI-era Vietnamese banking

Across global banks, AI has moved from pilots to scaled deployment. This session explores how AI is reshaping bank economics — from revenue growth and cost-to-serve to risk management and productivity — while examining the governance, liability and trust frameworks needed as autonomous workflows expand.

  • Where AI productivity is proving real, from KYC, fraud and collections to relationship management
  • How banks should approach agentic AI that can approve, decide or transact on behalf of customers
  • Liability, redress and trust when AI agents get it wrong
  • How bank and central bank leaders are thinking about AI, tokenisation and cross-border connectivity
Foo Boon PingPresident & Managing Editor, The Asian Banker
Deputy GovernorState Bank of Vietnam (SBV)
10:15 – 10:45

Networking Break

10:45 – 11:15
Panel Discussion

Panel Discussion

Will agentic AI grow the bank or eat its margin?

Agentic AI has moved past the demonstration stage. Multi-step agents are now executing collections, transaction monitoring, KYC enrichment and advisory drafting inside leading banks. This session focuses on the unit economics of agentic banking and the boundary between autonomous and human-led decisions.

  • Where agentic workflows are already shifting unit economics
  • Approval architecture: which decisions stay human, which go autonomous
  • How to think about deposit, advisory and merchant economics if customers prefer agent-to-agent finance
  • Vendor lock-in, model concentration, and the cost of running multiple frontier models in production
11:15 – 11:30
Presentation

Solo Presentation

What a production-grade AI banking stack looks like in 2026

Building a bank stack that can run agentic workflows safely at scale demands a composable core, an agent runtime, vector and graph data layers, observability, and an operating model that can put models into production without breaking compliance.

11:30 – 12:10
Panel Discussion

Panel Discussion

How payment data is rewriting SME credit in Vietnam

Vietnamese MSMEs remain the country's largest unmet credit opportunity, while payment data is reshaping how lenders assess risk and distribute credit. This session explores what this means for SME lending economics, distribution and competition.

  • How payment-data underwriting is changing default rates, ticket sizes and approval times
  • The economics of embedded SME lending through digital platforms
  • Where banks should partner versus compete with super-apps and digital lenders
12:10 – 12:25
Presentation

Solo Presentation

AI vs AI: defending the real-time bank against industrialised scams

Network-level AI controls have become essential for defending instant payment systems against industrialised scam operations, mule networks, deepfake-enabled identity attacks and synthetic identity at onboarding.

12:25 – 14:00

Networking Luncheon

14:00 – 14:30
Panel Discussion

Panel Discussion

Can AI-augmented RMs finally crack the mass-affluent wealth opportunity?

Vietnam's mass-affluent segment is a fast-growing source of wealth-banking revenue, yet it remains under-served. This panel explores how AI-augmented RMs, generative advisory drafting, and modern wealth platforms are being used to make the segment economically attractive at scale.

  • How AI-augmented RMs change advisory unit economics
  • Product shelf: fixed income, FX, multi-currency deposits, tokenised funds
  • Risk profiling and suitability when the advisor is partly an AI agent
14:30 – 15:00
Panel Discussion

Panel Discussion

Who pays when an AI scams the customer?

Industrial-scale APP scams, mule-account abuse and deepfake-based identity attacks have turned scam-fraud into a board-level P&L item. This panel examines who pays when the customer is scammed — the bank, the platform, or the customer.

  • Where AI-versus-AI defences have meaningfully reduced loss
  • The reimbursement question: voluntary, regulated or shared
  • Network-level intelligence sharing: privacy-enhancing analytics and cross-bank consortia
15:00 – 15:20

Networking Break

15:20 – 15:50
Panel Discussion

Panel Discussion

Tokenised deposits, stablecoins and Vietnam's CBDC pilot move from pilot to production

Digital money is moving from pilot to production, with tokenised deposits, stablecoins and the SBV's CBDC pilot creating new possibilities for payments, treasury and capital markets.

  • Tokenised VND and USD deposits as treasury and FX rails
  • The SBV CBDC pilot: where it sits in the next-generation monetary system
  • Tokenised securities and settlement
15:50 – 16:20
Closing Session

Closing Session

The Vietnamese banking CEO playbook for 2026–2027

The next 18 to 24 months will define winners and losers across Vietnamese and ASEAN banking. This session focuses on where to invest in 2026, the role of partnerships and M&A, and what ASEAN integration buys a Vietnamese bank by 2027.

  • Where to invest in 2026 if cost-to-income has to fall by 300–500 basis points
  • The role of partnerships, M&A and divestiture in a more concentrated digital landscape
  • What ASEAN integration realistically buys a Vietnamese bank by 2027
16:20

Closing Remarks
